# 🎬 Thine: Post-Production Guide

Follow these exact steps to edit your demo for maximum social performance.

## 🎵 Music Selection
Look for tracks with these keywords: **"Tech Noir," "Phonk," "Cyberpunk Bass," "Minimal Pulsating Synth."**

### Suggested Pacing:
- **0:03**: Micro-drop (bass kick) on the "MOST PEOPLE DON'T" text.
- **0:12**: Main Drop (beat entry) as the phone snaps into frame.
- **Maintain a constant 140-150 BPM** to keep the energy high.

---

## 🛠 Editing Workflow (CapCut / Final Cut Pro)

### 1. The Speed Ramp (The "Satisfying" Swipe)
- **Problem**: Natural screen recordings of swiping can feel sluggish.
- **Fix**: Use **Speed Rapping**. 
  - Start at **0.5x speed** as the finger touches the card.
  - Ramp up to **4x speed** as the card flies off-screen.
  - This makes the interaction feel "snappy" and premium.

### 2. Motion Blur
- Add a **Motion Blur** filter (Settings: 60-80% blur) to the card toss animations.
- Tech demos look "amateur" without blur on fast movements.

### 3. Subtitle Styling
- **Font**: Use a clean, bold Sans-Serif or the Thine Serif.
- **Animation**: "Glitch" or "Pop" animations for the hook text (Scene 1).
- **Position**: Center-screen for the hook; bottom-center for feature names.

### 4. Color Grading
- Crank the **Contrast** (+10) and **Sharpness** (+15).
- Deepen the blacks to make the `--bg` (#050505) look infinite.
- Add a subtle outer-glow to the phone frame to match our `--accent` (#f49752).

---

## 🎯 The "Infinite Loop" Trick
- Ensure the final frame (Logo) has the same background and light-level as the first frame (Black Screen).
- In CapCut: use a **Cross-fade** transition between the last 0.5s and the first 0.5s of the loop.
- This results in people watching the video 2-3 times before realizing it's over—instantly doubling your "View Completion" metric.
