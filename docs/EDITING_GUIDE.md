# Thine Editing Guide

This is the post-production guide for turning raw Thine screen captures into premium social assets.

It complements:
- `/Users/HP/thine/docs/DEMO_SCRIPT.md`
- `/Users/HP/thine/docs/MARKETING.md`

Use this doc when you are editing:
- short demo videos
- launch teasers
- product reels
- homepage motion pieces
- founder-led product walkthroughs

## 1. Creative Goal

Every edit should make Thine feel like:
- a premium product
- a sharp psychological mirror
- something social and repeatable, not disposable
- something that is both useful and a little addictive

The viewer should feel:
- `I want to know my score.`
- `I want to see my profile.`
- `I want to compare this with someone else.`

## 2. Visual Language

### Core look
Lean into the product’s current visual identity:
- near-black backgrounds
- warm orange accents
- restrained grid and glow treatment
- serif-led display moments for important reveals
- rounded panels with breathing room

### What to emphasize visually
- score reveals
- share cards
- swipe motion
- streak/XP moments
- crowd stats in Arena
- match card comparison

### What to downplay
- raw browser chrome
- long static form moments
- slow transitions that feel like generic SaaS
- empty space without narrative purpose

## 3. Capture Preparation

### Capture two device contexts
- desktop for legibility and product breadth
- mobile for social-native realism and share behavior

### Capture both anonymous and logged-in states
Why:
- anonymous shows the frictionless core loop
- logged-in shows dashboard, leaderboard, and compounding behavior

### Capture at least these product states
- homepage hero
- quiz personalization
- quiz question progression
- results report
- share button + toast
- full-analysis lock
- Arena swipes
- Arena summary
- crowd stats block
- match card
- leaderboard
- dashboard

## 4. Editing Rules

### Keep tempo intentional
Thine should feel deliberate, not frantic.

Good pacing:
- hook text: under 1 second if it is only one line
- core UI beats: 1 to 2 seconds
- major reveal moments: up to 2.5 seconds if visually strong

### Cut redundancy aggressively
If two shots communicate the same thing, keep the sharper one.

Examples of redundancy to remove:
- multiple similar question-answer taps
- repeated swipes without a summary payoff
- too many leaderboard rows
- multiple nearly identical share-card views

### Save breathing room for the important moments
Do not over-cut the parts that create value:
- score reveal
- report reveal
- arena profile reveal
- crowd stats
- match card
- final CTA

## 5. Motion Treatment

### For swipes
- add subtle speed ramps if the raw swipe feels slow
- keep gesture direction readable
- motion blur can help remove the “screen recording” feel

### For report reveals
- use a clean push, slide, or masked reveal
- avoid gimmicky glitch transitions unless they are extremely restrained

### For stat moments
Use small emphasis moves for:
- XP burst
- streak chip
- share toast
- leaderboard highlight

These should feel like product proof, not animation for animation’s sake.

## 6. Text Overlay Rules

### Keep overlays short
Target:
- 2 to 6 words for hook cards
- 1 sentence max for explanatory overlays

### Good overlay patterns
- `Memory isn’t a system.`
- `Get your score.`
- `Share your result.`
- `See your thinking profile.`
- `Challenge someone.`
- `Climb the leaderboard.`

### Avoid
- paragraphs
- repeated restatement of what the screen already says
- technical terms like `Supabase`, `RPC`, or `localStorage`

## 7. Typography Guidance

### Product-first approach
When using text overlays, choose typography that feels aligned with the product:
- elegant serif for statement hooks and major reveals
- clean sans-serif for support labels and subtitles

### Hierarchy
- hero line: large and dramatic
- support line: smaller and cleaner
- CTA: clear, not oversized

### Subtitle guidance
- always assume many viewers are muted
- keep subtitles concise
- avoid placing subtitles where they compete with the product UI

## 8. Color And Grade

### Recommended grade
- deepen blacks slightly
- keep orange accents warm, not neon
- avoid oversaturating the UI
- maintain readable contrast on text

### Overall mood
Think:
- dark editorial tech
- premium but not glossy
- controlled, not chaotic

## 9. Sound Design Guidance

### Best fit
- minimal electronic
- dark synth pulse
- crisp click/whoosh accents
- modern and clean, not trailer-heavy

### Good sound moments
- CTA tap
- share toast
- score reveal
- swipe resolution
- final brand hit

### Avoid
- comedic meme sounds
- overblown cinematic bass drops
- anything too playful for the product’s tone

## 10. Best Sequence Order Right Now

If you want the safest current edit structure, use this order:

1. hook line
2. problem framing
3. quiz start
4. report reveal
5. share/challenge moment
6. Arena swipes
7. Arena profile + crowd/match moment
8. leaderboard/dashboard
9. final CTA

This sequence reflects the actual shape of the platform better than showing only the quiz.

## 11. Export Guidance

### Social exports
- vertical: 1080x1920
- square: 1080x1080
- horizontal: 1920x1080 or 1200x630 for landing/support usage

### Compression rule
Do not crush the blacks so much that the UI becomes muddy. Thine depends on subtle tonal contrast.

## 12. Quality Checklist

Before publishing, confirm:
- the hook is clear in under 2 seconds
- the product looks intentional, not cluttered
- both Diagnostic and Arena appear
- at least one share/progression moment appears
- the final CTA is obvious
- subtitles are readable on mobile
- no single shot overstays its value

## 13. Common Editing Mistakes

- making it look like only a one-time quiz
- hiding Arena entirely
- showing too much dead space or scroll time
- overusing generic tech motion presets
- burying the share moment
- ending without brand + CTA

## 14. If You Need One Editing Principle

Cut the video so the viewer feels this:

> This is not just another quiz. It measures something real, gives me something worth sharing, and gives me a reason to come back.
