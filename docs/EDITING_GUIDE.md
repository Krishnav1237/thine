# Thine: Post‑Production Guide

Follow these steps to cut a tight, premium demo for social.

## Music Selection
Look for tracks with keywords: “Tech Noir,” “Phonk,” “Cyberpunk Bass,” “Minimal Pulsating Synth.”

### Suggested Pacing
- **0:03**: Micro‑drop on the hook line.
- **0:12**: Main drop as the phone snaps into frame.
- **140–150 BPM** to keep energy high.

---

## Editing Workflow (CapCut / Final Cut Pro)

### 1) Speed Ramp
- **Problem**: Raw swipe footage feels slow.
- **Fix**: Use speed ramping.
  - Start at **0.5x** as the finger touches the card.
  - Ramp to **4x** as the card exits.
  - Keeps motion snappy and premium.

### 2) Motion Blur
- Add motion blur (60–80%) to the card toss.
- Removes the “screen‑recording” feel.

### 3) Subtitle Styling
- **Font**: Clean, bold sans‑serif or Thine Serif.
- **Animation**: Simple “Pop” for the hook.
- **Position**: Center for hook, bottom‑center for feature labels.

### 4) Color Grading
- Increase contrast and sharpness slightly.
- Deepen blacks to match `--bg` (#050505).
- Add a subtle glow to the phone frame to match `--accent` (#f49752).

---

## The Seamless Loop
- Match the final frame lighting to the first frame.
- Use a 0.5s cross‑fade between end and start.
- The result feels infinite and boosts re‑watch rate.
