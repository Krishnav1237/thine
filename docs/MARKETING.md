# Thine Marketing Playbook

This document captures how Thine should currently be positioned in public-facing messaging.

It is designed to help with:
- product positioning
- launch copy
- founder-led content
- lifecycle messaging
- share copy direction
- channel-specific creative strategy

## 1. Core Positioning

### Plain-language description
Thine is a Personal Intelligence Platform that helps people measure how well they retain context, follow through on commitments, and stay aware of the people and work that matter.

### Short version
- `See how you actually work.`
- `Memory isn’t a system.`
- `Get a real score for follow-through, context, and recall.`

### Expanded positioning
Thine is not just a quiz and not just a swipe app.
It is a two-part product:

- a diagnostic that turns invisible operating habits into a measurable score
- an arena that turns instinctive reactions into a shareable thinking profile

Then it compounds both through:
- streaks
- XP
- ranks
- leaderboard visibility
- challenge loops
- public share moments

## 2. What Problem We Are Selling

### Core problem statement
People think they are “organized enough,” but most of their meaningful context is trapped in memory and decays quickly.

That creates downstream pain:
- forgotten follow-ups
- weak relationship continuity
- repeated decisions without context
- fuzzy recall before important conversations
- too much reliance on inbox search and luck

### What Thine reframes
Thine reframes that pain from vague self-improvement into something measurable, visible, and social.

- follow-up debt
- relationship state drift
- too many important threads, too little recall

Best hook:
- `Your memory is doing work your system should be doing.`

### Audience 2: PMs, designers, and knowledge workers
Pain points:
- fragmented context
- too many meetings
- weak note discipline
- unclear relationship or project state

Best hook:
- `Measure how much context your current workflow actually preserves.`

### Audience 3: social-first curious users
Pain points:
- want something identity-driven
- want fast and expressive interactions
- want something more interesting than a generic quiz

Best hook:
- `Find out how you think in Hot Takes Arena.`

## 5. Message Hierarchy

When writing homepage copy, posts, or videos, keep the message hierarchy in this order:

1. reveal something real
2. make it measurable
3. make it shareable
4. make it repeatable

In practice, that means:
- lead with the score or profile outcome
- then explain the system beneath it
- then show the progression and social proof layer

## 9. Share Copy Direction

### What good share copy should do
- be understandable in under 2 seconds
- make the result feel personal
- imply comparison or challenge
- feel native to social, not enterprise software

### What to avoid
- long explanatory copy
- overly technical copy
- generic “copy link” language in user-facing marketing surfaces
- fake social claims

### Preferred verbs
- share
- challenge
- compare
- unlock
- see
- measure
- reveal

## 10. Things We Should Not Accidentally Market Incorrectly

Do not say:
- Thine has a backend of crowd knowledge if the claim implies all scoring is crowdsourced
- the quiz uses external data to generate the score
- Arena percentages are fake mock data
- every feature requires account creation
- full analysis unlocks automatically without share/challenge behavior

Do say:
- the quiz produces a deterministic score from your answers
- Arena now shows real crowd response data when available
- logged-in users get persistence, leaderboard presence, and durable shares
- anonymous users can still use the core product end-to-end

## 11. Current Brand Tone
