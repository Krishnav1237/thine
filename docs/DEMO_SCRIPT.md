# Thine Demo Script

This document gives you a full demo structure for recording or editing Thine into a social-first product video.

The goal of the demo is not to explain every feature. The goal is to make viewers immediately understand three things:

- this product reveals something real about them
- it has more than one way to hook them
- it turns the result into something worth sharing or repeating

## 1. Core Narrative

The strongest current Thine narrative is:

1. you are leaking context
2. Thine measures that leak
3. Thine gives you a score, plan, and social proof loop
4. Thine also shows how you think in a second, faster mode
5. the product compounds over time through streaks, XP, rank, and leaderboard visibility

## 2. Recommended Demo Formats

### 20–25 second cut
Best for:
- X
- Instagram Reels
- short paid creative tests

Focus on:
- quiz hook
- report reveal
- arena flash
- share moment
- leaderboard proof

### 35–45 second cut
Best for:
- product teaser
- founder posts
- landing-page embeds

Focus on:
- quiz
- results report
- arena
- streak / XP / leaderboard
- account save angle

### 50–60 second cut
Best for:
- product walkthrough
- investor/product-context clips
- pinned social content

Focus on:
- both loops
- progression layer
- share system
- dashboard / leaderboard credibility

## 3. Recommended 45-Second Script

### 0:00–0:03 — Hook
Visual:
- black or near-black frame
- fast type reveal: `MEMORY IS NOT A SYSTEM.`
- subtle orange accent pulse

Voice/text:
- `Most people are running their professional life on memory.`

### 0:03–0:08 — Problem
Visual:
- quick cuts of meetings, inbox, follow-ups, half-remembered context
- scroll across the landing page hero
- tap into quiz CTA

Voice/text:
- `And most of that context disappears faster than they think.`

### 0:08–0:15 — Diagnostic Start
Visual:
- personalization screen
- session mode toggle from Quick to Deep
- question progression with one-tap answers

Voice/text:
- `Thine turns that into a score in about two minutes.`

Overlay copy ideas:
- `Quick 10 or Deep 20`
- `Daily rotation`
- `Personalized focus`

### 0:15–0:24 — Results Reveal
Visual:
- report card loading in
- score and band
- strengths / weakness
- share-card preview
- XP burst / streak / rank badge

Voice/text:
- `You don’t just get a result. You get a report, a playbook, and a reason to come back.`

Overlay copy ideas:
- `Personal Intelligence Score`
- `3-day plan`
- `Share your result`

### 0:24–0:32 — Viral / Challenge Loop
Visual:
- tap share button
- toast feedback
- challenge ref flow
- locked full analysis state
- highlight that friend completions unlock more depth

Voice/text:
- `Share your score, challenge someone, and unlock more of the analysis as people join in.`

### 0:32–0:40 — Arena Switch
Visual:
- switch to Arena
- rapid swipes and taps
- summary reveal with thinking profile
- crowd stats line
- match card reveal

Voice/text:
- `Then jump into Hot Takes Arena to see how you actually think under pressure.`

Overlay copy ideas:
- `Daily 7 or Avid 12`
- `Crowd response`
- `Your match`

### 0:40–0:45 — Progression + Brand
Visual:
- leaderboard row
- dashboard snapshot
- final brand frame with URL

Voice/text:
- `Score it. Share it. Compound it.`

End card:
- `Thine`
- `See how you actually work.`

## 4. Shot List

### Must-have shots
- homepage hero
- session mode toggle in quiz
- at least 2–3 quiz questions
- results page with score and report
- share action and toast confirmation
- full-analysis lock state
- arena swipes
- arena summary with thinking profile
- crowd stats or match card if available
- leaderboard
- dashboard

### Nice-to-have shots
- auth modal
- daily check-in card
- XP animation
- streak badge in navbar
- rank tier badge
- durable share page preview

## 5. Capture Guidance

### Capture both desktop and mobile
Why:
- desktop sells the product surface clearly
- mobile sells real user behavior and shareability

### Capture three specific flows
1. anonymous user completes quiz
2. anonymous user completes arena
3. logged-in user lands on dashboard / leaderboard

### Record with intentional variety
Do not capture only perfect, static screens.
Get:
- taps
- swipes
- hover states if desktop
- toasts
- mode switches
- auth modal opening
- page transitions

## 6. On-Screen Copy Library

Use these short lines as overlays instead of inventing new copy every time.

### Problem / hook
- `Memory is not a system.`
- `Most context dies after the moment.`
- `Your follow-through is measurable.`

### Quiz
- `Quick 10. Deep 20.`
- `Measure memory, follow-up, consistency, awareness.`
- `Get a real score.`

### Results
- `Your Intelligence Report.`
- `Strengths. Weakness. Next steps.`
- `Retake in 3 days.`

### Arena
- `How do you actually think?`
- `Swipe your stance.`
- `See your profile.`

### Progression
- `Stack XP.`
- `Build your streak.`
- `Climb the leaderboard.`

### Share
- `Share your result.`
- `Challenge someone.`
- `Can they beat you?`

## 7. Editing Rhythm

### Recommended rhythm by section
- hook: 0.5–1.5 second cuts
- quiz: 1–2 second cuts
- results: let the score breathe for 1.5–2 seconds
- arena: faster again, especially on swipes
- final proof/brand: 1.5–2 seconds

### What to linger on
Only linger on moments that create perceived value:
- score reveal
- playbook reveal
- crowd stats
- match card
- leaderboard row

### What to trim aggressively
- long scrolls
- empty loading gaps
- repeated swipes with no payoff
- forms sitting still too long

## 8. Audio Direction

### Good sound profile
- dark electronic
- minimal percussion
- modern tech-noir vibe
- controlled build, not chaotic EDM

### Pacing suggestion
- subdued tension for first 5–8 seconds
- build on quiz flow
- lift on report reveal
- tighter rhythm for arena
- clean hit on final brand frame

## 9. CTA Options

Use one of these depending on the asset.

### Diagnostic-first CTA
- `Get your score.`
- `Take the diagnostic.`

### Arena-first CTA
- `See your thinking profile.`
- `Play Hot Takes Arena.`

### Platform CTA
- `See how you actually work.`
- `Score it. Share it. Compound it.`

## 10. Mistakes To Avoid

- do not show too much text at once
- do not over-explain Supabase, auth, or backend mechanics in the creative
- do not spend the whole demo on one loop; show both quiz and arena
- do not make the product feel like only a one-time quiz
- do not end without a clear CTA or brand frame
